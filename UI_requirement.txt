UI:
Then you're aiming for what many of the best B2B companies optimize for: design that disappears. Users shouldn't think "nice UI"; they should think "this is easy."
Companies like Stripe, Linear, Notion, Vercel, GitHub, and Figma all follow this philosophy.
1. Colors: Use less color than you think
I'd actually reduce color usage.
Primary: #0F766E
Accent: #F59E0B

Background: #FAFAF9
Surface: #FFFFFF

Text:
Primary: #1C1917
Secondary: #57534E
Muted: #A8A29E

Border: #E7E5E4

Success: #16A34A
Error: #DC2626
The primary color should appear in only about 5–10% of the interface.
Most enterprise apps are 90% grayscale.
________________________________________
2. Radius
Instead of
6px
I'd recommend
8px
8px has become a modern default. It feels polished without becoming playful.
________________________________________
3. Shadows
Keep them extremely subtle.
box-shadow:
0 1px 2px rgba(0,0,0,.04);
No large floating cards.
The separation should come from whitespace first, shadows second.
________________________________________
4. Borders > Shadows
Enterprise UI relies heavily on borders.
1px #E7E5E4
rather than
box-shadow: 0 10px 50px ...
________________________________________
5. Spacing
This matters more than colors.
Use an 8-point spacing system:
4
8
16
24
32
48
64
96
Never use random values like 13px or 27px.
________________________________________
6. Typography
Geist is a great choice.
A clean scale:
H1 36
H2 30
H3 24
H4 20

Body 16

Small 14

Caption 12
Prefer medium (500–600) over bold unless emphasis is necessary.
________________________________________
7. Buttons
Keep them simple.
Primary:
Teal background
White text
No gradient
No glow
Secondary:
White background
Gray border
Dark text
Ghost:
Transparent
No border
Avoid pill-shaped buttons.
________________________________________
8. Icons
Use Lucide with:
strokeWidth={1.5}
or
1.75
This feels lighter and more refined.
________________________________________
9. Animations
Minimal.
•	150–200ms duration
•	Ease-out
•	Fade + slight movement (2–4px)
•	No bounce
•	No elastic effects
Users should notice smoothness, not the animation itself.
________________________________________
10. Information Density
Enterprise products often balance density and readability:
✓ Plenty of whitespace
✓ Compact tables
✓ Clear hierarchy
✓ Consistent alignment
✓ Few decorative elements
Don't make users scroll through oversized cards if they need to work with data.
________________________________________
11. Remove these common startup trends
Avoid:
•	❌ Glassmorphism
•	❌ Neon gradients
•	❌ Huge blurred backgrounds
•	❌ Heavy drop shadows
•	❌ Floating cards everywhere
•	❌ Colorful icons by default
•	❌ Large hero illustrations unless they add meaning
•	❌ Overuse of animations
________________________________________
12. UI Principles to follow
Think in terms of usability before aesthetics:
•	Consistency: identical components behave identically.
•	Visual hierarchy: size, weight, and spacing guide attention.
•	Progressive disclosure: show advanced options only when needed.
•	Recognition over recall: use labels and familiar patterns instead of making users remember.
•	Clear feedback: every action should have an obvious result.
•	Accessible contrast: maintain strong readability and obvious interactive states.
•	Generous click targets: around 44×44px for interactive controls where appropriate.
•	Predictable layouts: users should rarely wonder where to look next.
________________________________________
The overall aesthetic
Your design language should feel like this:
"Professional. Quiet. Precise. Intentional."
Not:
"Look at our beautiful UI."
The best enterprise interfaces become almost invisible because they're consistent, restrained, and predictable. If you keep color, motion, and decoration minimal while investing heavily in spacing, typography, and interaction quality, you'll end up with a design that feels premium for years instead of chasing short-lived trends.
